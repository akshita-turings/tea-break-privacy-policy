Child Safety Standards — Tea Break

Published by TURINGS XYZ PRIVATE LIMITED — Last updated:

Zero tolerance for Child Sexual Abuse & Exploitation (CSAE)

TURINGS XYZ PRIVATE LIMITED and the Tea Break team have a zero-tolerance policy for any content, activity or behaviour that promotes, depicts, distributes, or facilitates Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE), including Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM). All reports of CSAE or possible CSAM are taken seriously and handled according to law and best practice.

(This statement is published to comply with the Google Play Child Safety Standards requirement that publicly-available standards explicitly prohibit CSAE.)

What we prohibit

CSAE / CSAM
Any content that depicts, sexualizes, exploits, or endangers minors — including text messages, stickers, GIFs, links, audio recordings, or any other form of content — is strictly forbidden.
Sexual contact with minors
Any facilitation, arrangement, or encouragement of sexual contact with minors (including grooming) is strictly prohibited.
Distribution or sharing of exploitative materials
Sharing, linking to, or encouraging distribution of exploitative materials or illegal contact is prohibited and will result in immediate action (suspension, removal, and law-enforcement notification where required).

How we detect and respond

We combine automated detection, moderator review, and user reports to identify and remove CSAE/CSAM quickly. Our response includes:

  • Immediate removal of violating content.
  • Account suspension or termination for users who share or promote CSAE/CSAM.
  • Preservation of evidence and notification to law enforcement when required by law.
  • Cooperation with relevant authorities and compliance with applicable child-safety laws and obligations in jurisdictions where we operate.

In-app reporting and user feedback

Users can report suspected CSAE, CSAM, grooming, or any child-endangering behaviour via the in-app report mechanism: In the Tea Break app, navigate to a user's profile → Tap the "Report" button → Enter your reason for reporting → Tap "Send". Reports are routed to our moderation team and reviewed 24/7 where possible.

For immediate safety concerns, users can also use the SOS/Safety feature available in audio rooms to report feeling unsafe.

Google requires an in-app feedback/report mechanism for apps in scope — ensure your app actually exposes a working report flow.

Moderation, training, & escalation

Our moderators receive training on child safety and CSAE detection. Moderation steps include triage, preservation of evidence, escalation to senior reviewers, and referral to law enforcement for criminal matters. We also maintain retention logs to support lawful investigations.

Legal compliance

TURINGS XYZ PRIVATE LIMITED complies with applicable local laws and regulations concerning child protection, reporting, and data preservation. Where required, we will follow lawful requests and local obligation to report suspected CSAE to public authorities.

Accountability & enforcement

Violators of this policy are subject to immediate content removal, account suspension or termination, and referral to law enforcement. Reinstatement (if any) will be subject to investigation and guarantees that the user will not re-offend.

Transparency & review

We review these standards periodically and will update them as necessary to reflect changes in law and industry best practice. This page is public and provides the standards used to moderate content and behaviour on Tea Break.
